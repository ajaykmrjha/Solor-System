The Sun







The Sun orbits the center of the Milky Way galaxy at a distance of approximately
26,000 light-years from the galactic center, completing one revolution in about 225–
250 million years. The orbital speed is 217 km/s (135 mi/s), equivalent to one light-
year every 1,400 years, and one AU every 8 days. It is currently travelling through
the Local Fluff of the Local Bubble zone of diffuse high-temperature gas, in the
inner rim of the Orion Arm of the Milky Way Galaxy, between the larger Perseus
and Sagittarius arms of the galaxy.
